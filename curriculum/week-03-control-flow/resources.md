# Week 3 — Resources

A short, curated reading list. Everything here is either free or has a free
sample chapter that covers what we need. You do not have to read all of it
in one week — pick the format that suits how you learn and keep the rest
bookmarked for later.

## Official Python Documentation (free)

- **More Control Flow Tools** — the canonical reference for `if`, `for`,
  `while`, `break`, `continue`, `else` on loops, and `pass`. Read this at
  least once, even if it feels dense:
  <https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/controlflow.html>
- **The `range` type** — exactly how `range(start, stop, step)` works and
  why it is *not* a list in modern Python:
  <https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#range>
- **Truth Value Testing** — the authoritative list of which built-in values
  are considered false:
  <https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#truth-value-testing>
- **Boolean operations** — how `and`, `or`, `not` short-circuit and what
  they actually return (hint: not always `True`/`False`):
  <https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#boolean-operations-and-or-not>
- **Built-in `enumerate()`**:
  <https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#enumerate>
- **Built-in `zip()`**:
  <https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#zip>

## Free Online Books

- **Automate the Boring Stuff with Python** by Al Sweigart — Chapter 2,
  *Flow Control*. A beginner-friendly walk-through of `if`/`elif`/`else`,
  `while`, `for`, `break`, and `continue` with lots of small examples.
  Free to read online at <https://automatetheboringstuff.com/2e/chapter2/>.
- **Think Python (2nd ed.)** by Allen B. Downey — Chapter 5 (*Conditionals
  and Recursion*) and Chapter 6 (*Fruitful Functions* — skim for `while`
  and recursion at the end). Free PDF and HTML at
  <https://greenteapress.com/wp/think-python-2e/>.

## Style and Philosophy

- **PEP 8 — Style Guide for Python Code**, especially the
  *Indentation* and *Programming Recommendations* sections. Pythonic
  control-flow choices come straight from here:
  <https://peps.python.org/pep-0008/#indentation>
- **PEP 20 — The Zen of Python**: type `import this` into the REPL or read
  it at <https://peps.python.org/pep-0020/>. "Flat is better than nested"
  is a guard-clause justification you will quote in code reviews.

## How to Use These

1. **Watch or read** one introductory resource for `if`/`else` (Automate
   the Boring Stuff Ch. 2 is the easiest entry point).
2. **Work through** our [lecture notes](./lecture-notes/) — they assume you
   skimmed the intro above.
3. **Look up** the official docs whenever something surprises you. Building
   the habit of reading docs early pays off forever.
